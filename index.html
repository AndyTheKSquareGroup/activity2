KINCHIL

About Kinchil

Kinchil is located in the territory that was the jurisdiction of the Ah Canul before the conquest of Yucatán.

No precise information is known about the founding of the town before the conquest of Yucatan by the Spaniards. It is known, however, that during the colony it was under the regime of the encomiendas, among which we can mention Pedro Castellanos and Petrona Magaña Dorantes.

In 1825, after the independence of Yucatan, Kinchil was part of the Camino Real Bajo Party, with its capital in Hunucmá. When the Organic Law of the Municipalities of the State of Yucatan came into effect in 1918, Kinchil became the head of the free municipality of the same name.

Who was Felipa Poot?

Felipa Poot, a Mayan leader, was gunned down by a gunman of the PNR senatorial candidate, Gualberto Carrillo Puerto.

elipa Poot, despite her young age, had a distinguished career as a defender of the rights of women and the Mayan people, and had organized a literacy program for adults working in the fields of western Yucatán. She was also president of the "Rita Cetina" Feminist League, founded by Elvia Carrillo Púerto, in her native Kinchil.

On January 4, 1936, Felipa Poot Tzuc's name became known in Yucatán when she traveled to the state capital to demand that Governor Fernando López Cárdenas dismiss the mayor of Kinchil, Juan de Mata Solís, who was linked to a group that had gained power after the large landowners were displaced during the Revolution.

On the afternoon of March 28, 1936, Felipa confronted a man named Juan Méndez, a supporter of Carrillo Puerto; in front of several people, the woman stripped him of a knife, causing his public humiliation. Felipa, upon approaching the corner of 20th and 25th streets, was shot at.

The figure of Felipa challenges the patriarchal order that not even the Revolution had managed to overthrow.
